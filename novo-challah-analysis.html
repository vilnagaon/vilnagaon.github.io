Why Challah Is the #1 Application

Levartos AA CL + Challah

Challah is a high-sugar, high-fat enriched dough — exactly where dough conditioners earn their money. Sugar and fat weaken gluten structure by competing for water and coating gluten strands. Levartos compensates with improved gas retention and dough strength, producing taller, more impressive braided loaves with better oven spring.

  • Fermentation tolerance — Critical for bakeries producing hundreds of challahs on a Friday schedule. A 15-minute delay between batches won’t ruin the dough
  • Machinability — Challah dough is typically hand-braided, but dividing and rounding benefit from improved dough handling
  • Volume — Higher gas retention = taller braids with more defined strands
  • Ultra-low inclusion — At 0.0625% flour weight, the cost per challah is negligible

Novosoft LS + Challah

Challah’s #1 consumer complaint is staling. Baked on Friday morning, it’s often noticeably drier by Shabbat lunch — just 24 hours later. By Sunday, it’s toast material. Novosoft LS directly addresses this:

  • Rich bread = 28 days softness — Challah is one of the richest yeast breads (eggs, oil, sugar, honey). It gets the maximum benefit from Novosoft’s enzyme system
  • No early gumminess — Some anti-staling enzymes (particularly amylases) cause a gummy, wet crumb in the first 24 hours. Novosoft’s formulation avoids this, delivering a clean crumb from hour one
  • Shabbat timing — Kosher bakeries bake challah Thursday night or Friday morning for sale on Friday. The bread must be perfect for Friday night dinner and Saturday lunch. Extended freshness covers this window with room to spare
Key Insight: The combination of both products addresses the two biggest challenges in commercial challah production: (1) dough consistency under time pressure, and (2) freshness across the 24–48 hour Shabbat window. Together, they deliver a challah that handles better, rises taller, and stays soft longer — with nothing but wheat flour, enzymes, and ascorbic acid on the label.
